            Summary: This gene encodes the D4 subtype of the dopamine receptor.
            The D4 subtype is a G-protein coupled receptor which inhibits
            adenylyl cyclase. It is a target for drugs which treat
            schizophrenia and Parkinson disease. Mutations in this gene have
            been associated with various behavioral phenotypes, including
            autonomic nervous system dysfunction, attention
            deficit/hyperactivity disorder, and the personality trait of
            novelty seeking. This gene contains a polymorphic number (2-10
            copies) of tandem 48 nt repeats; the sequence shown contains four
            repeats. [provided by RefSeq, Jul 2008].
