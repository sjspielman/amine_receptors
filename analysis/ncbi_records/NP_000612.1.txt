            Summary: This gene encodes one of the receptors for serotonin, a
            neurotransmitter with many roles. Mutations in this gene are
            associated with susceptibility to schizophrenia and
            obsessive-compulsive disorder, and are also associated with
            response to the antidepressant citalopram in patients with major
            depressive disorder (MDD). MDD patients who also have a mutation in
            intron 2 of this gene show a significantly reduced response to
            citalopram as this antidepressant downregulates expression of this
            gene. Multiple transcript variants encoding different isoforms have
            been found for this gene. [provided by RefSeq, Sep 2009].
