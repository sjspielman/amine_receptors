            Summary: Histamine is a ubiquitous messenger molecule released from
            mast cells, enterochromaffin-like cells, and neurons. Its various
            actions are mediated by a family of histamine receptors, which are
            a subset of the G-protein coupled receptor superfamily. This gene
            encodes a histamine receptor that is predominantly expressed in
            haematopoietic cells. The protein is thought to play a role in
            inflammation and allergy reponses. Multiple transcript variants
            encoding different isoforms have been found for this gene.
            [provided by RefSeq, May 2009].
